## üìù Form Fields Explained

### 1. ‚õΩ Fuel Amount (Liters) - REQUIRED
**What to Enter:** The number of liters you pumped into the vehicle

**Where to Find This:**
- Look at the fuel pump display after filling
- Check the receipt - shows liters/gallons
- Example: 50.5 liters

**Why It's Needed:**
- Calculate fuel efficiency (km per liter)
- Track if vehicle is consuming too much fuel
- Verify the cost matches the amount

**Tips:**
- Enter the exact amount from the pump
- Use decimal point for accuracy (50.5, not 50)
- Don't round up or down

---

### 2. üíµ Cost ($) - REQUIRED
**What to Enter:** Total amount you paid for the fuel

**Where to Find This:**
- Look at the fuel pump display (total cost)
- Check the receipt - final amount paid
- Example: $75.00

**Why It's Needed:**
- Company needs to reimburse you (if you paid)
- Track fuel expenses for budgeting
- Compare prices across different stations
- Required for accounting and tax records

**Tips:**
- Enter the exact amount from receipt
- Include cents (75.00, not 75)
- Don't include other purchases (snacks, car wash)

---

### 3. üõ£Ô∏è Current Mileage (km) - REQUIRED
**What to Enter:** The vehicle's odometer reading AFTER fueling

**Where to Find This:**
- Look at the vehicle's odometer (dashboard)
- It shows total kilometers driven since vehicle was new
- Example: 45,230 km

**Why It's Needed:**
- Calculate fuel efficiency (km per liter)
- Track vehicle usage and wear
- Schedule maintenance based on mileage
- Verify you're not exceeding allowed mileage
- Detect odometer tampering

**Important Notes:**
- The form shows the last recorded mileage
- Your new entry MUST be higher than the last entry
- Example: If last entry was 45,000 km, you must enter at least 45,001 km
- This prevents errors and fraud

**Tips:**
- Check odometer AFTER filling fuel (not before)
- Enter the exact number you see
- Don't estimate or guess
- Take a photo of odometer if unsure

---

### 4. üìç Gas Station Location - REQUIRED
**What to Enter:** Name and location of the fuel station

**Examples:**
- "Shell Station, Main Street"
- "Petron, Highway 101 North"
- "Caltex, Downtown Branch"
- "BP Station, Airport Road"

**Why It's Needed:**
- Verify fuel purchase is legitimate
- Track which stations have better prices
- Identify if driver is going off-route
- Required for receipt verification
- Helps other drivers find good stations

**Tips:**
- Include station brand name (Shell, Petron, etc.)
- Add street name or landmark
- Be specific enough to find it on a map
- Match the location on your receipt

---

### 5. üì∑ Receipt Photo - OPTIONAL (but recommended)
**What to Upload:** Photo of the fuel receipt

**Why It's Needed:**
- Proof of purchase for reimbursement
- Verify the amount and cost you entered
- Required for accounting audits
- Prevents disputes about expenses
- Company policy may require it

**How to Add:**
1. Click "üì∑ Add Receipt Photo"
2. Choose:
   - **Take Photo** - Use camera to photograph receipt now
   - **Choose from Library** - Select existing photo

**Tips for Good Receipt Photos:**
- Make sure receipt is flat and readable
- Good lighting - no shadows
- All text is visible (date, amount, station name)
- Don't fold or crumple receipt
- Take photo immediately after purchase (receipts fade)

**What Should Be Visible on Receipt:**
- Station name and location
- Date and time
- Fuel amount (liters)
- Price per liter
- Total cost
- Payment method

## ‚úÖ Validation Rules

The form will NOT let you submit if:

1. **Fuel amount is 0 or empty**
   - Error: "Please enter a valid fuel amount"
   - Fix: Enter the liters from your receipt

2. **Cost is 0 or empty**
   - Error: "Please enter a valid cost"
   - Fix: Enter the total amount paid

3. **Mileage is less than last recorded mileage**
   - Error: "Mileage must be at least [X] km"
   - Fix: Check your odometer again - it should be higher than last time

4. **Location is empty**
   - Error: "Please enter the fuel station location"
   - Fix: Enter the station name and location

## üéØ Step-by-Step: How to Fill the Form

### At the Gas Station:

**Step 1: Before Fueling**
- Note your current odometer reading (optional, for your reference)

**Step 2: During Fueling**
- Fill the tank
- Watch the pump display

**Step 3: After Fueling**
- Note the odometer reading (IMPORTANT!)
- Get the receipt
- Note the fuel amount (liters)
- Note the total cost

**Step 4: In the App**
1. Open Vehicle Information screen
2. Click "‚õΩ Log Fuel Entry"
3. Enter fuel amount from receipt (e.g., 50.5)
4. Enter total cost from receipt (e.g., 75.00)
5. Enter current odometer reading (e.g., 45,230)
6. Enter station location (e.g., "Shell Station, Main Street")
7. (Optional) Take photo of receipt
8. Review the summary - check if price per liter looks correct
9. Click "Save Entry"

## üí° Common Questions

### Q: What if I don't have the receipt?
**A:** You can still submit without a photo, but:
- You MUST enter accurate amount and cost
- Company may ask for receipt later
- Better to take photo immediately to avoid issues

### Q: What if I forgot to note the mileage?
**A:** 
- Go back to the vehicle and check the odometer
- Don't guess - enter the exact number
- If you already drove away, check odometer before next stop

### Q: What if I filled fuel at multiple stations?
**A:**
- Create separate entries for each station
- Each entry should have its own receipt
- Mileage should increase with each entry

### Q: What if someone else paid for fuel?
**A:**
- Still log the entry
- Enter the amount and cost from receipt
- Company tracks fuel consumption even if they paid directly

### Q: What if the pump shows gallons, not liters?
**A:**
- Convert gallons to liters: 1 gallon = 3.785 liters
- Example: 13.3 gallons √ó 3.785 = 50.3 liters
- Or check receipt - usually shows both

### Q: Can I edit an entry after submitting?
**A:**
- Currently NO - entries cannot be edited
- Double-check all information before clicking "Save Entry"
- If you made a mistake, contact your supervisor

### Q: How often should I log fuel?
**A:**
- EVERY time you fill fuel
- Don't skip entries
- Don't combine multiple fill-ups into one entry
